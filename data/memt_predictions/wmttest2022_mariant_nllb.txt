The International Olive Oil Association divides olive oil into two major categories, five levels, of first-fried olive oil (Virgin Olive Oil) and of olive oil (Lampante Olive Oil or Refined Olive Oil).
The first-time production of olive oil, or what is known as natural olive oil, is the fat obtained directly from fresh olive fruit through mechanical cooling, filtration, etc.
There are three levels depending on the acidity:
Extra Virgin: The highest and highest-quality olive oil and pure natural product.
The taste is excellent, the flavor of the plant is sweet, and the acidity is not more than 1%.
Fine Virgin: slightly higher but not more than 2% acid, pure and aromatic.
Ordinary olive oil (Ordinary Virgin): The taste and taste are good, and the acidity does not exceed 3.3 per cent.
A refined olive oil is an olive oil obtained as a result of refining the primary olive oil, which is more than 3.3 per cent acidic, or becomes a “secondary oil”.
The refined olive oil can be divided into two levels:
Olive Oil: A refined olive oil mixed with a certain proportion of primary olive oil to match the flavor and color, with an acidity of less than 1.5% and a clear pale yellow.
Refined Olive-Pomace Oil: Olive oil extracted from the oil slag and refined by solution.
If you have looked at the ranking, you should have noticed that the acidity of olive oil is a very important indicator.
It is important to note that olive oil, like grapes made from fermented wine, is produced from different regions, different olive varieties, and different standards of olive oil.
Best growing ground for olive oil:
The Mediterranean coast has the most suitable soil and climatic conditions for growing olive oil, such as temperature, sunlight, humidity and rainfall.
With the exception of the Mediterranean coast, other areas can only be cultivated sporadically in suitable areas.
Main areas of production (Mediterranean):
Italy, Spain, Greece, Turkey, Portugal, Morocco, Syria, Israel, Tunisia, Algeria, Egypt, Pakistan, Lebanon, etc. The climate is similar to that of Argentina, Peru, South Africa, etc. in the far south latitudes.
The month of extraction of olive oil:
Between March and June of each year, the olive trees bloom and the fruits begin to grow and mature from July to October, turning from green to red, purple and, finally, black.
November to February is the fruit harvest season (the freshness of olive oil is important).
The nutritional content of olive oil:
Olive oil is rich in monounsaturated fatty acids, namely oleic and oleic acids, and amethylamine acids, as well as vitamins A, B, D, E, K, and antioxidants.
Olive oil is considered to be the most nutritious oil of all the fats found to date.

Article 18 The Department of Public Safety Fire Department is the registered approval department for the registration of fire engineers.
The public security agencies of the provinces, autonomous regions and municipalities directly under the central authority are the competent authorities for the registration and approval of the qualifications of fire engineers registered at the secondary level and are responsible for the initial review of the qualifications of fire engineers registered at the first level.
Article 19 Persons who have obtained a certificate of qualification as a registered fire engineer of first and second class and who apply for registration shall be employed by an approved fire technical service institution or a fire safety priority unit and submit registration application materials through the hiring unit to the public safety fire service institution of the unit's location (employee-owned enterprise, registered with the business through the enterprise).
Article 20 The fire departments of the public safety authorities of the provinces, autonomous regions and jurisdictions shall, after receipt of the registration application material of the applicant, be informed of the incomplete or unlawful form of the application material on the spot or within five working days of the date of receipt of the application material, without delay, of all the information required to be corrected by the applicant.
Any application for registration that is admissible or inadmissible shall be certified in writing with a stamp and a date for the exclusive use of the fire service of the public security organ of this level.
Article 21 Fire-fighting agencies of provincial, autonomous and directly administered municipalities shall, within 20 working days from the date of receipt of the application for registration, complete the preliminary review of applications for the registration of fire engineers at the first level and the registration and approval of the qualifications of fire engineers at the second level, in accordance with the prescribed conditions and procedures, and report the application for the registration of fire engineers at the first level to the Fire Department of the Ministry of Public Security for approval and approval.
The Department of Public Safety shall decide whether to approve the fire department within 20 working days of receipt of the application materials and preliminary review opinions submitted by the provincial fire departments.
The approval decision cannot be taken within the prescribed period, and the extension of the period and the reasons for the extension shall be notified to the applicant.
The reasons for the decision not to be approved shall be stated in writing and the applicant shall be informed of the right to seek administrative review or to bring administrative proceedings under the law.
Article 22 Registration and approval authorities shall issue and service the appropriate level of registration within 10 working days of the date on which the approval decision is taken.
Article 23 Each registration certificate is valid for three years.
The registration certificate, which is valid for the period of validity, is a certificate of practice for a fire engineer registered at the appropriate level and is kept and used by the registered fire engineer himself.
Article 24 Where an application for initial registration is made, the application shall be made within one year from the date of obtaining the certified first- and second-level fire engineer qualification.

Specific procedures for the administration of registration, such as initial registration, renewal of registration, change of registration, cancellation of registration and non-registration, are regulated separately by the Ministry of Public Security.
Article 25 Continuing education is a prerequisite for the continuing registration, re-registration and overdue initial registration of registered fire engineers.
During each registered life cycle, registered fire engineers at all levels shall complete the corresponding continuing education as required.
Article 26 Registration and approval authorities should register fire engineers in a timely manner in a social notice, establish a record of the integrity of the registered fire engineers and exercise credit management over their professional activities.
